The meeting at the local club started with a heated debate about game design history. Our host, Stefan, argued that modern cooperative game designs have completely revolutionized the industry. To prove his point, we sat down to play Pandemic, the famous title by Matt Leacock. Management of the crisis was stressful; we had exactly 4 players, and it took us 55 minutes to find the cure. The game requires exceptional hand management, which is a mechanism I usually struggle with when my hand is full of cards. 

Right after that, we wanted to test a classic area control game, so we unboxed an old, forgotten prototype titled Dice Rolling, which was released by an unknown indie studio back in 2022. It was a funny paradox: even though the game was literally called "Dice Rolling", the core gameplay actually didn't feature any dice rolling mechanics at all! Instead, it relied on a strict action points system where you spend 3 resource cards to move a token. The box stated a minimum playing time of 90 minutes, but due to the heavy complexity, our session lasted for over two hours. 

We finished the night with Unstable Unicorns from Breaking Games. We used the 2021 edition and mixed it with the Unstable Unicorns: Nightmares Expansion Pack. It was a brutal competitive game where constant take that actions and merciless player elimination destroyed all friendships at the table. Someone noted that while the overall BGG rating is 7.4, the hidden traitor element in the expansion makes it feel much more strategic than a standard party game. It’s a wild ride, heavily recommended for anyone who fits the suggested age of 14+ and can handle a complexity level of 2.00 out of 5.
